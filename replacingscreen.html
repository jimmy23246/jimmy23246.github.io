Replacing iPhone Screen

As a part of ECE1100 Individual Project, I envisioned a project to replace the broken OLED screen of my old iPhone X. This was done by using tools bought- and following the guideline- from iFixit.com, and the replacement screen purchased from ebay.com.

Before the restoration, the corner of the screen is darkened due to dead OLED pixels.

Step 1

Remove the two T5 torque screw on the bottom of the casing.

Step 2

Using the heat gun, or a hair dryer, apply heat thoroughly onto the screen to melt the water seal. 
Using the opening tool, carefully remove the screen without damaging the connect on the top of the screen.

Step 3

After, removing the sealant, carefully open up the screen to the right-side of the case. 
Be careful not to damage the screen/Face ID connector.

Step 4

Using the T6 screw driver, remove the plastic cover which covers the connector for the screen.
Remove the screen connector located at the very top of the plastic cover portion.

Step 5

Remove the FaceID connector and camera connector, and fully remove the screen from the phone.
